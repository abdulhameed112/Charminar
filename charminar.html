Explore History

A Monument of Legacy

Built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, the Charminar stands as an enduring symbol of Hyderabad's rich heritage. This magnificent structure was constructed to commemorate the founding of the city and to mark the end of a deadly plague that had ravaged the region.

The name "Charminar" translates to "Four Towers" in Urdu, perfectly describing its iconic four-minaret design. Each minaret rises 56 meters high, creating a perfect square structure that has become synonymous with Hyderabad's identity.

Beyond its architectural beauty, Charminar serves as the gateway to the old city's bustling bazaars, where centuries-old traditions of craftsmanship continue to thrive.
